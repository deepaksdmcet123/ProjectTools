Karnataka Tourism

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace is a historical palace and a royal residence in Mysore, Karnataka. It is known for its grandeur and Indo-Saracenic architecture.

Hampi

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient temples, ruins, and breathtaking landscapes.

Coorg

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a hill station famous for its coffee plantations, scenic beauty, and waterfalls.

Gokarna

Gokarna is a small coastal town known for its pristine beaches and as a pilgrimage site.

Bandipur National Park

Bandipur National Park is a wildlife sanctuary known for its tigers, elephants, and lush forests.

Gokak falls

Gokak Falls: Gokak Falls, often referred to as the "Niagara of Karnataka," is a stunning waterfall located on the Ghataprabha River. The waterfall drops from a height of 170 feet, creating a magnificent sight. It is famous for its picturesque beauty, surrounded by lush greenery and rocky terrain. A historic hanging bridge nearby adds to the charm of the falls, making it a popular spot for tourists and photographers. The best time to visit Gokak Falls is during the monsoon when the water flow is at its peak, and the surrounding landscape looks vibrant and alive.

nandi hills

Nandi Hills: Nandi Hills is a popular hill station and historical site located near Bangalore, Karnataka. It is known for its breathtaking views, cool climate, and lush green landscapes. The hill is named after the Nandi Bull, as it resembles the shape of a reclining bull. Nandi Hills offers a serene escape from the bustling city and is famous for its sunrise views, ancient temples, and fortifications. The Nandi Temple at the peak is a significant religious spot. Trekking to the top and enjoying the panoramic vistas makes it an ideal destination for nature lovers, photographers, and adventure enthusiasts.

madikeri

Madikeri: Madikeri, also known as the "Coorg," is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Western Ghats of Karnataka. Famous for its scenic coffee plantations, dense forests, and cool weather, it is a paradise for nature lovers. Madikeri offers visitors a tranquil retreat, with attractions like Abbey Falls, Raja’s Seat, and the Madikeri Fort, which are all rich in history and natural beauty. The region is also known for its wildlife and trekking trails, making it a great destination for adventure enthusiasts. Madikeri is the perfect place to unwind, sip some freshly brewed coffee, and explore the beauty of Coorg.

Jog falls

Jog Falls: Jog Falls, located in the Shimoga district of Karnataka, is one of the tallest waterfalls in India. This majestic waterfall is formed by the Sharavathi River and plunges down from a height of 830 feet, creating a spectacular sight. The falls split into four distinct cascades—Raja, Rani, Rover, and Rocket—which adds to its unique charm. Jog Falls is best visited during the monsoon season when the water flow is at its peak, transforming the falls into a magnificent force of nature. The surrounding landscape, with its lush greenery and misty atmosphere, makes it an ideal spot for photography and nature walks.
